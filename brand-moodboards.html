Brand Do's & Don'ts
Do
Use generous whitespace — let the brand breathe
Pair serif headlines with sans-serif body text
Use gold sparingly — it's the accent, not the base
Keep photography editorial, natural light, minimal
Write with quiet authority — no exclamation marks
Lead with data and insight, follow with invitation
Don't
Overuse gold — it loses its power when everywhere
Use all-caps for body text — only for labels & tags
Mix more than 2 typefaces in one layout
Use casual or "girlboss" language
Put text on cluttered backgrounds
Use drop shadows, gradients, or 3D effects

Tone of Voice
Warm, not soft
We're friendly and approachable — but we back every claim with data. Warmth with substance.
Clear, not clever

Brand Do's & Don'ts
Do
Use the gradient as a hero moment — sparingly
Round all corners — buttons, cards, images (12-20px)
Leave generous whitespace around content
Use light mode for documents, dark for digital
Write like you're talking to a smart friend

Don't
Put gradient text on gradient backgrounds
Use sharp corners or square buttons
Mix the gradient with additional brand colours
Use serif fonts anywhere in the system
Write in corporate-speak or passive voice
Sacrifice readability for visual impact

Voice & Tone
Alive, not abstract
Use concrete metaphors from nature. Growth, roots, blooming, soil. Show that inclusion is a living process, not a checkbox.
Proud, not arrogant
We stand tall like the tulip. We know our value. But we grow from the same soil as everyone else.
Rooted, not rigid
Our methodology is evidence-based and proven. But we adapt to every client, every market, every context.
Colourful, not chaotic
Each petal has its purpose. Vivid expression, but always in service of clarity. Beautiful and functional.
Brand Do's & Don'ts
Do
Use the petal colours meaningfully — each represents a methodology step
Let Fraunces italic shine in headlines and pull quotes
Use organic, rounded shapes (16-24px border-radius)
Reference Dutch heritage subtly — tulips, fields, growth
Mix warm and cool tones for energy and balance
Pair vivid photography with restrained typography
Don't
Use all five petal colours in one composition — pick 2-3 max
Make tulip references too literal — no clipart flowers
Use pastel, washed-out colours — Bloom is vivid
Default to feminine stereotypes — pink doesn't mean soft
Overcrowd layouts — let the colours breathe
Lose the Dutch connection — it grounds the whole identity

The Inclusive Business Collective · 2026
Voice & Tone
Transparent, not naive
We show everything — the data, the gap, the opportunity. Clarity is our weapon and our gift.
Luminous, not flashy
We illuminate what's hidden. We don't perform — we reveal. Quiet brilliance over loud claims.
Layered, not complex
Like glass panels stacking: each layer adds depth, but you can always see through to the core.
Breaking, not broken
We shatter ceilings, not spirits. The metaphor is about power and progress, never victimhood.
Brand Do's & Don'ts
Do
Use frosted glass consistently — every card, panel, overlay
Let spectrum colours appear as light, not paint (accents, not fills)
Use soft, generous border-radius (24-32px)
Add luminous glows behind glass panels for depth
Keep backgrounds deep and dark — glass needs darkness to shine
Use the spectrum gradient for key moments only
Don't
Use solid spectrum colours as backgrounds — they're light, not pigment
Show literal broken glass — it reads as violence, not empowerment
Make glass panels too opaque — transparency is the point
Use more than 2 spectrum colours per composition
Forget the metaphor — every design choice ties back to seeing through
Put glass effects on light backgrounds — they disappear
