# Gender Capital Readiness — Self-Assessment

**Who fills this in:** each member of the client crew, independently, before the sprint (T–7 → T–2).
**How long:** ~15 minutes.
**Why:** this *is* the Readiness Scan. It produces the first-draft readiness percentage per dimension that the Diagnostic Engine refines with the data room. Independent answers surface where the organisation disagrees with itself — which is where the opportunity usually hides.

> Answer for **how it actually is today**, not how it should be or how a project once aspired to be. "I don't know" is a real and useful answer — score it 0.

This version is tuned for a **pension provider (BeFrank)**. The five dimensions are Unlockt's standard analytical backbone; the questions are localised to pensions, participants, employers, and the app.

---

## How to score

Each statement is rated **0–4**:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **0** | Not true / we don't do this / I don't know |
| **1** | Ad hoc, isolated, one person's initiative |
| **2** | Happening in parts, inconsistent, not measured |
| **3** | Consistent and measured, not yet driving decisions |
| **4** | Systematic, measured, and it changes what we do |

Average each dimension's scores, then convert to a **readiness percentage**: (average ÷ 4) × 100, rounded. The overall score is the average of the five dimension percentages. Scores are always presented as **rings** — a circle whose border fills to the percentage (50% = half-encircled).

| Readiness score | Band | Reads as |
|---|---|---|
| 80 – 100% | **Koploper** | Leading — gender-aware by design |
| 60 – 79% | **In opbouw** | Building — aware, partially systematic |
| 35 – 59% | **Latent** | Pockets of activity, no system |
| 0 – 34% | **Blinde vlek** | Opportunity untouched |

The score is never the point. The **lowest two dimensions with the highest commercial upside** are the point — that's where the sprint aims.

## Dimension 1 — Employee & Organisation
*Bias in our own people, incentives, and decision systems.*

1. Our product, data, and marketing teams can name the gender pension gap figure for our own book. `0–4`
2. Closing the gender outcome gap appears in someone's actual targets/KPIs (not just a values statement). `0–4`
3. When we design a default (contribution, investment profile), we check its differential effect on women vs men. `0–4`
4. We have the internal capability (or a partner) to run a gender-lens analysis without starting from scratch. `0–4`
5. Leadership treats the women's pension gap as a commercial topic, not only an ESG/reporting topic. `0–4`
6. Decisions about scheme design consider part-time and career-break patterns explicitly. `0–4`

## Dimension 2 — Data
*Can we even see the gap? Revenue, funnel, and outcome data by gender.*

1. We can split our participant book by gender × age × accrued pension × contribution rate today. `0–4`
2. We track app engagement (Mijn Pensioen logins, choices made) by gender. `0–4`
3. We can see where women drop off or stay passive in key journeys (onboarding, contribution choice, value transfer). `0–4`
4. We measure the *projected* retirement-income gap for our female participants, not just current accrual. `0–4`
5. We capture life events (hours change, parental leave, salary change) as data we can act on. `0–4`
6. We benchmark our gap against peers / national figures. `0–4`

## Dimension 3 — Marketing & Communications
*Do our messages reach, fit, and move women participants?*

1. Our onboarding and life-event communications are tested for how they land with women specifically. `0–4`
2. We intervene with a message at the moments that widen the gap (going part-time, parental leave). `0–4`
3. Our tone and examples assume diverse life paths, not a default full-time linear career. `0–4`
4. Campaigns are measured on whether they changed women's *behaviour* (contributions, choices), not just reach. `0–4`
5. Marketing/comms staff have been trained to spot gendered assumptions in their own copy. `0–4`
6. We can run a continuous scan of our communications for these blind spots, not a one-off audit. `0–4`

## Dimension 4 — Ecosystems
*Employers, advisors, and partners — the channel to the participant.*

1. We give employers data/tools on the gender pension gap in their own workforce. `0–4`
2. Advisors who sell/administer our schemes are equipped to raise the gap with employers. `0–4`
3. We partner with organisations women trust at the relevant life moments. `0–4`
4. Employer onboarding includes a conversation about closing the gap, not just cost and compliance. `0–4`
5. We have referral/partner routes that specifically reach under-served participants. `0–4`

## Dimension 5 — Propositions
*Do our products and defaults actually fit women's pension reality?*

1. We offer a product, default, or feature explicitly designed to mitigate the part-time/career-break penalty. `0–4`
2. Our defaults are set so that *doing nothing* still narrows the gap rather than widening it. `0–4`
3. Participants get a clear, personalised view of their projected gap and what closes it. `0–4`
4. We make it easy to act at the moment it matters (top up around parental leave, adjust on an hours change). `0–4`
5. We have tested at least one gap-closing proposition with real participants. `0–4`
6. A participant would say our proposition "suits them" regardless of their career shape. `0–4`
